Boost Your Writing Productivity: Accountability and Peer Support Opportunities

Managing your writing projects, alongside the many other responsibilities you have, can be challenging whether or not there’s a pressing deadline. To help faculty meet their writing goals, the Faculty Center for Professional Excellence offers two key initiatives:

  • Writing Accountability Groups: These groups provide faculty with dedicated time and a structured, supportive space to focus on their writing. Open to faculty across disciplines, participants work on a variety of projects, including grant submissions, research articles, tenure materials, or creative writing pieces. Through bi-weekly, synchronous Zoom (and/or in-person) sessions, faculty can build momentum and productivity while benefiting from a consistent and focused writing environment.
  • Writing Peer Review and Support Groups: Designed for faculty seeking collaborative feedback on writing projects, including grant proposals, these bi-weekly groups (held synchronously via Zoom and/or in-person) offer a facilitated environment for peer support. Participants receive constructive insights and encouragement from their colleagues, helping them refine their work and overcome challenges.

Whether you’re looking for uninterrupted writing time or collaborative feedback, these initiatives foster a culture of productivity and mutual support among faculty writers.
