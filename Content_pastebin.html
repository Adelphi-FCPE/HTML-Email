The Faculty Center for Professional Excellence is pleased to offer the 10-day Online Academy: Course Development Intensive this Summer. The start date for the Online Academy will be June 17; the program will run on weekdays through June 28.

The Online Academy has been designed to run as an asynchronous faculty professional development program, with:

  • Daily readings, activities, and assignments which were created as applied practice in course development for online or hybrid sections.
  • Optional synchronous workshops/labs that address online pedagogy, technology, and accessibility.
The Online Academy allowed me to conceptualize a course fully but then take apart each piece (like a puzzle), learn about it, analyze it, and see how it all fits together. Putting in the time through the Academy made for a much smoother course.
— Sara Terrana, Assistant Professor in the School of Social Work

Faculty participants will be cohorted in small groups to facilitate community building and collaboration. The cohorts will be led by FCPE Instructional Designers.

Upon successful completion of the Online Academy (85% or better), participants will be awarded a certificate featuring credentials backed by Accredible.

“Like many faculty members, I enrolled in the Online Academy during the early days of the pandemic because I needed to modify my class content for online and remote teaching. Even as I teach in-person classes now, I am able to thoughtfully design online activities, lesson plans, and assessments that support in-person instruction... I highly recommend faculty taking this course.”

— Anthony Dotterman, Associate Teaching Professor Adelphi Plus, College of Arts and Sciences
