Day 8: Accessibility Online

Coming up on Day 8

You’ve spent hours tracking your course design, from both its overall to modular learning objectives. You identified and began designing key assessments/assignments, nevermind the effort spent thinking through how to build community and reflect that community and policies in your syllabus! Now, it’s time to ensure the fruits of your labor can be accessed by all students.

Accessibility ensures that all learners can fully participate in and benefit from a course. According to the National Center for Education Statistics, around 21% of undergraduates and 11% of post-baccalaureate students have reported having a disability. However accessibility is not only beneficial to students with disabilities; history has shown that accessible design benefits everyone. Consider:

  • Temporary or situational disabilities (ie. recent injury or the environment in which the student completes their work)
  • Nontraditional older students
  • Non-native English speaking students
