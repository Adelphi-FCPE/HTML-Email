Day 7: Syllabus Revision

Coming up on Day 7

Welcome to Day 7: Syllabus Revision. As we continue to move through our ADDIE steps, we turn our attention to the development of an effective syllabus, with special consideration to policies that best fit an online/hybrid course environment. The role of the syllabus within Higher Education is quite versatile: some students see it as a contract and others, a simple schedule of expected due dates. Indeed, a syllabus is versatile. Its functions are introductive, informational, contractual, and referential; as it must introduce you as the instructor, inform your students of essential class details, work as a contract for expectations, and serve as a reference for course materials/deadlines.

Within an online or hybrid course, it must also work as a self-explanatory text. In face-to-face learning, instructors can clarify questions and build rapport with students in real-time (building that asynchronous engagement and community we spoke about yesterday!). However in an online environment, the syllabus must take on a greater role in fostering classroom culture and expressing clear, concise information.

After reviewing the module content, you will work on updating and developing your syllabus. Remember! Your syllabus will be a digital first impression, so consider the policies and language that will welcome students to both you and your online course!
