Day 3: Backward Design Part 2

Coming Up on Day 3

As you continue to map and align your Course's Learning Objectives (CLOs), Module Learning Objectives (MLOs), module titles, materials, activities, and assessments, you'll need to consider the NYSED requirements for teaching online, and think about/translate contact hours to the online modality.

To get started with Backward Design, we've provided resources, worksheets, rubrics, and templates to help you align your Course-Level Learning Objectives (CLOs) with Modular Learning Objectives (MLOs).

To help you do this, we have provided resources that will assist you in better understanding time on task, including resources that allow you to estimate how much time you should allot to instructional materials, activities and assessments, as well as the benefits of using a course map to help you develop and design your course.

For today’s assignment, we want you to put all of your developing expertise on Backward Design and Time on Task to work by continuing to develop a chosen module in your course by now adding time on task estimates for your instructional materials, activities and assessments and of course, continuing to consider how all of the above align with your MLOs.

As always, feel free to reach out to us if you have any questions.
